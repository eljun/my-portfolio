Obedient and Holiness

Author: Eleazar Junsan

1 Peter 1:14-16

obedient children, not fashioning yourselves according to the former lusts in your ignorance: But as he which hath called you is holy, so be ye holy in all manner of conversation; Because it is written, Be ye holy; for I am holy.

The verse above thought us about (2) two essential things they were:

  • Obedience
  • Holiness

1) When we talk about obedience and holiness what could come first in our mind?

2) It is possible to achieve these on our current sinful state?

Paul penned down that there is none righteousness? For all have sinned and come short of the Glory of God? Romans 3:10, 23?

"But they have not all obeyed the gospel. For Esaias saith, Lord, who hath believed our report?” Romans 10:16

The answer is NO, definitely NO, because to be carnally minded is enmity with God. – Romans 8:7 and in verse 9 if a man has not the Spirit of Christ he is none of His."

The Lord Jesus Christ

Yes, what we need the Spirit of Christ the spirit of obedience as he had already shown us the way, which way should we go LORD? Says Thomas and he Christ answered “I AM THE WAY, THE TRUTH, and THE LIFE”.

If Christ is the WAY then we should follow Him and make Him as our guide?

And some people make conclusion that to believe in Jesus is all that it takes to be saved, somehow this is true as the Bible tells us so.

John 3:16

For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life."

But the question is what does the Bible say or literally mean about “Believing”?

Philippians 1:29

For unto you it is given in the behalf of Christ, not only to believe on him, but also to suffer for his sake;”
  • So “believing” also means to suffer for His sake, because James warns us that “Faith without work is dead” James 2:17
  • “Blessed [are] they which are persecuted for righteousness' sake: for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.” Matthew 5:10
  • Believing also means to believe on something not visible “Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.” - Hebrews 11:1

Christ had promised us something very important before His ascension and that we should believe on that promised as well.

The Comforter

John 14:26

But the Comforter, [which is] the Holy Ghost, whom the Father will send in my name, he shall teach you all things, and bring all things to your remembrance, whatsoever I have said unto you.”

Ephesians 1:17-18

The God of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Father of glory, may give unto you the spirit of wisdom and revelation in the knowledge of him: The eyes of your understanding being enlightened; that ye may know what is the hope of his calling, and what the riches of the glory of his inheritance in the saints…”

1 Corinthians 2:10

But God hath revealed [them] unto us by his Spirit: for the Spirit searcheth all things, yea, the deep things of God. For what man knoweth the things of a man, save the spirit of man which is in him? even so the things of God knoweth no man, but the Spirit of God.”

Now we can get the idea the reason why Paul said in Romans 8:7 that “If a man do not have the Spirit of Christ he is none of His”? Because it is the WORK of the Spirit through faith that we may again gain the knowledge of our Father, because the Spirit search the deep things of God and will bring to our remembrance the former things the warning, reproof, and corrections for righteousness.

John 16:8

It is the Spirit that condemns us of our sins “And when he is come, he will REPROVE the world of SIN, and of righteousness, and of judgment”

The fruit of Spirit

Therefore we need the Spirit of Christ to bring us to repentance, because only the through the spirit that man can render complete obedience to the claims of God. – Romans 8:4

If the spirit of God dwells IN US it brings forth good fruit they were, “LOVE, JOY, PEACE, LONGSUFFERINGS, GENTLENESS, GOODNESS, FAITH…” – Galatians 5:22

  • We will be able to fulfill the whole duty of man – Ecclesiastes 11:13
  • We will be able to keep His commandments – 1 John 5:3
  • Discernment what is good and right – Malachi 3:18
  • Open Understand they that they understand the scripture – Luke 24:45

Encouragement

We should be steadfast, unmovable, to keep his commandments without spot and unrebukeable until the appearing of our Lord Jesus.

1 Corinthian 15:58

Therefore, my beloved brethren, be ye steadfast, unmovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, forasmuch as ye know that your labour is not in vain in the Lord."

1 Timothy 6:14

That thou keep [this] commandment without spot, unrebukeable, until the appearing of our Lord Jesus Christ:”
